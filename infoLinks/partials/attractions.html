Attractions

A traditional, picturesque, tranquil yet vibrant town, Bunclody lays on the scenic border of counties Carlow and Wexford. Bunclody truly has something for everyone – from hiking, hang gliding, fishing, golfing, a wide variety of restaurants, and much more. Pubs / bars are plentiful and you’ll hardly find a warmer welcome.

Mount Leinster

The highest peak in the Blackstairs Mountains, Mt Leinster (796m) has magnificent views of counties Waterford, Carlow, Kilkenny and Wicklow from the top.

Altamont Gardens

Widely respected as the jewel in Ireland’s gardening crown, Altamont Gardens are an enchanting blend of formal and informal gardens with riverside walks covering over 40 acres (16 hectares). The estate gains much of its character from the many mature trees, natives in the avenues and woodland areas, and exotic specimens throughout the gardens. Lawns bisected by sculpted yews slope down to a lake surrounded by rare trees and rhododendrons and lead in turn to a very different garden featuring exotic shrubs and trees.A fascinating walk through the Arboretum, Bog Garden and Ice Age Glen with its canopy of ancient oaks and huge stone outcrops leads the visitor to the River Slaney. Newtownbarry House and Garden

Just over 1km walk away from the Moss House, through a picturesque forested path one will find the Newtownbarry House. Enjoy lunch and tea while surrounded by beautiful landscapes, gardens and a large pond adjacent to the entrance of the house. Follow the tour of photos from the Lake to the Rose Garden, and on to the recently renovated Nineteenth Century Sunken Garden. Source: www.newtownbarryhouse.com/housegardens/gardens/intro.aspx
